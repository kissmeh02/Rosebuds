Terpene Guide

Understand the aromatics that shape your experience.

What Are Terpenes?

Terpenes are aromatic compounds found in cannabis (and many other plants) that give each strain its unique smell and flavor. More importantly, they influence the effects you feel — a concept known as the entourage effect.

Limonene Citrus, Lemon

Uplifting and stress-relieving. Found in citrus fruits. Often associated with elevated mood and anti-anxiety properties.

Found in: Velvet Citrus, Sunrise Haze Cart

Myrcene Earthy, Musky

The most common cannabis terpene. Promotes relaxation and sedation. Also found in mangoes, hops, and lemongrass.

Found in: Lavender Kush, Nightfall Cart, Midnight Garden

Caryophyllene Spicy, Peppery

The only terpene that binds to CB2 receptors. Known for anti-inflammatory properties. Found in black pepper and cloves.

Found in: Velvet Citrus, Emerald Coast, OG Classic

Linalool Floral, Lavender

Calming and sedating. The same terpene that makes lavender relaxing. Useful for anxiety and sleep support.

Found in: Lavender Kush, Nightfall Cart, Midnight Garden

Pinene Pine, Fresh

Promotes alertness and memory retention. The most common terpene in nature — it's what makes forests smell fresh.

Found in: Pine Mist, Emerald Coast, Midnight Garden

Terpinolene Herbal, Floral

Complex and nuanced. Mildly sedating with uplifting properties. Found in nutmeg, tea tree, and cumin.

Found in: Midnight Garden, Velvet Citrus

Dosing 101

Find the right amount for a comfortable experience.

Start Low, Go Slow

The golden rule of cannabis dosing. Everyone's tolerance is different, and it's always easier to take more than to undo too much.

Microdose
1–2.5mg THC

Subtle mood lift without impairment. Great for beginners, daytime use, or productivity. You might not "feel" much — that's the point.

Try: Focus Mint Drops (1mg/drop)

Low Dose
2.5–5mg THC

Mild euphoria and relaxation. Good for social settings, light anxiety relief, or winding down. Most people feel something at this range.

Try: Berry Calm Gummies (2.5mg/pc)

Moderate
5–15mg THC

Stronger euphoria and body effects. Recommended for regular users. Impairment is likely — don't drive.

Try: Mango Bliss Chews (5mg/pc)

Strong
15–30mg+ THC

Intense effects. Only for experienced users with established tolerance. Onset for edibles: 30min–2hrs.

Try: Consult our team first

Edible timing tip: Edibles take 30 minutes to 2 hours to kick in. Wait at least 2 hours before taking more. Inhaled products (flower, vapes) are felt within minutes.

Indica vs Sativa vs Hybrid

What the labels really mean and how to choose.

Understanding Strain Types

While modern science shows the indica/sativa distinction is more nuanced than simple categories, these labels remain useful shorthand for the general effects you can expect.

Sativa
Energizing
  • Uplifting, cerebral effects
  • Boosts creativity and focus
  • Best for daytime use
  • May increase energy

Best for: Social events, creative projects, outdoor activities

Our picks: Velvet Citrus, Sunrise Haze Cart

Hybrid
Balanced
  • Mix of mind and body effects
  • Varies by specific strain
  • Versatile — any time of day
  • Can lean indica or sativa

Best for: Versatile use, moderate relaxation with mental clarity

Our picks: Emerald Coast, Midnight Garden, Cloud Nine

Indica
Relaxing
  • Calming, full-body effects
  • Promotes relaxation and sleep
  • Best for evening/nighttime
  • May reduce pain and tension

Best for: Winding down, sleep support, pain management

Our picks: Lavender Kush, Nightfall Cart, OG Classic

Pro tip: Terpene profiles are actually a better predictor of effects than indica/sativa labels. Check the terpene guide to learn which aromatics produce the effects you want.

What are terpenes and why do they matter?

Terpenes are aromatic compounds that shape the flavor and effects of each strain. They work with cannabinoids to create what's known as the "entourage effect." Check our Terpene Guide for a detailed breakdown.
